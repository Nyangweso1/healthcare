SHA (Social Health Authority) Complete Guide - 2026

As of January 2026, the Social Health Authority (SHA) has fully replaced NHIF as the primary public health insurance provider in Kenya. Under the new Universal Health Coverage framework, registration is a legal requirement for every resident. Whether you are salaried, self-employed, or a student, access to government-subsidized healthcare now depends on your SHA status.

Method 1: Quick USSD Registration (*147#)

Dial *147# to start the automated SHA enrollment flow. You will provide your National ID number and verify your name against the IPRS database.

  • Fastest option for initial registration
  • Works on basic phones without internet
  • You will still need the portal later for dependents and profile completion
Method 2: Afya Yangu / SHA Portal (Full Registration)

For complete setup, use afyayangu.go.ke or sha.go.ke.

  1. Enter your ID number and a phone number registered in your name.
  2. Verify your account using the OTP sent to your phone.
  3. Create a secure 4-digit PIN.
  4. Select your county, sub-county, and nearest health facility.

This is also where you complete Means Testing, an AI-driven assessment used to determine monthly contributions for non-salaried households.

The 2.75% Contribution Rule
  • Salaried members: Mandatory deduction of 2.75% of gross monthly salary
  • No upper cap: Higher earners contribute more to the risk pool
  • Informal sector minimum: KES 300 per month
Method 3: Add Dependents Correctly

Family cover only applies after dependents are properly linked to your SHA profile.

  • Spouse: Marriage certificate or sworn affidavit
  • Children under 18: Birth certificate or birth notification number
  • Children 18-25: Proof of school enrollment (student ID or registrar letter)

If dependent records are incomplete, facilities may deny family treatment even when your own contribution is active.

Linda Mama Maternity Program

Free Maternity Care for All

Linda Mama is a government program providing FREE maternity services to all Kenyan women, regardless of insurance status.

What's Covered (100% FREE):
  • All antenatal clinic visits
  • Normal and caesarean deliveries
  • Postnatal care up to 6 weeks
  • Complications management
  • Newborn care
How to Access:

Simply visit any public health facility with your national ID. No registration or payment required!

Family Insurance Plans

Cover Your Entire Family

Family packages cover you, spouse, and up to 6 children under one policy - often cheaper than individual plans!

What to Look For:
  • Dependents covered: Spouse + children (usually up to 21 years)
  • Cost savings: 30-50% cheaper than separate policies
  • Shared benefits: Pool annual limits across family
Recommended Providers:
  • SHA Family Plan: KES 2,000-4,000/month (government)
  • Britam Family: KES 8,000-20,000/month (private)
  • Jubilee Family: KES 7,000-18,000/month (private)

Chronic Illness Coverage

Managing Diabetes, Hypertension & More

If you have a chronic condition, specialized coverage is essential:

Best Providers for Chronic Conditions:
  • CIC Insurance (54+ enrollments): Specialized chronic disease management programs
  • SHA: Covers essential chronic disease medications
  • APA: Good coverage for ongoing treatments
What to Check:
  • Annual medication limits (minimum 100,000 KES)
  • Coverage for specialist consultations
  • Laboratory tests included
  • Pre-existing condition waiting periods

Community-Based Insurance

Cooperative Health Schemes

Community-based health insurance (CBHI) is perfect for informal workers and rural residents:

How It Works:
  1. Join a local health cooperative (ask at your county health office)
  2. Pay low monthly premiums (KES 300-1,000)
  3. Access healthcare at designated facilities
  4. Community members support each other
Benefits:
  • Very affordable premiums
  • No complex paperwork
  • Community support
  • Basic but essential coverage
